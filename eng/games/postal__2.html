Postal 2

  Postal 2 is a 2003 first—person shooter video game developed by Running with Scissors. It is a sequel to the 1997 Postal game and was released for Microsoft Windows in April 2003, macOS in April 2004 and Linux in April 2005. Postal 2, like its predecessor, gained notoriety for its high levels of violence, stereotypes, and dark humor. Unlike the first game, Postal 2 is played in the first person. The game takes place in the fictional city of Paradise, based on the real city of Bisbee. Postal 2 tells the story of a Dude who must perform mundane tasks during the game week, while the player decides how aggressively or passively he will react to various situations. The player moves through the open world, fulfilling his duties, and the player's choice affects the situation. Upon release, the game received mixed reviews from critics. The game received several expansion packs, and in December 2003, a multiplayer expansion called Postal 2: Share the Pain was released. The game has been constantly updated, and in April 2015, a new expansion called Paradise Lost was released. Postal 2 became Linux Game Publishing's fastest-selling game in its first month and made a major contribution to the company's continued profitability. The game attracted attention with its violent gameplay and caused a lot of controversy. The game was followed by a sequel. Postal III is released in December 2011.
