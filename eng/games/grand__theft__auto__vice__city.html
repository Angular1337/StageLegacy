Grand Theft Auto: Vice City

  Grand Theft Auto: Vice City is a thirdâ€”person action-adventure computer game developed by British studio Rockstar North and released by American studio Rockstar Games. It is the fourth main game and the second three-dimensional game in the Grand Theft Auto series. The game was released on October 29, 2002 for the PlayStation 2 console and on May 12, 2003 for personal computers. The Austrian studio Rockstar Vienna also ported the game to Xbox, releasing the collection Grand Theft Auto: Double Pack, which also includes the previous game in the series, Grand Theft Auto III. On January 4, 2008, the game became available for download via the Steam Internet service. In honor of the tenth anniversary of the game's release, on December 6, 2012, Rockstar released a special version of the game for mobile devices running the iOS and Android operating systems. Porting was handled by the American company War Drum Studios. On January 29, 2013, a ported version of the game was released from PlayStation 2 to PlayStation 3 and distributed via the PlayStation Network. On November 11, 2021, Rockstar Games released a remastered compilation of Grand Theft Auto: The Trilogy is The Definitive Edition, which includes GTA III, GTA: Vice City and GTA: San Andreas. The game takes place in the fictional American city of Vice City, which was modeled on the real city of Miami in 1986. The visual style of the game (especially clothing, music, and transportation) reflects this, sometimes with elements of elegant parody. The protagonist of the game is Tommy Vercetti, who became a victim of the conspiracy of his criminal boss Sonny Forelli. The game's concept and gameplay, combined with the use of the game's 3D engine, helped make Grand Theft Auto: Vice City the best-selling video game of 2002 and one of the most profitable computer games in history.
